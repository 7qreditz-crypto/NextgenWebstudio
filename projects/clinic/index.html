Dermatologist Tips

Expert Skincare Advice

Always Wear SPF 50+ — Even Indoors
UV radiation is the single greatest cause of premature skin aging and skin cancer. UVA rays penetrate glass and cloud cover year-round. Broad-spectrum SPF 50+ is the non-negotiable foundation of every skincare routine, reapplied every 2 hours outdoors. Pair with oral Polypodium leucotomos extract for added protection. This one habit prevents up to 80% of visible aging signs over a lifetime.
01
🌙
Retinoids at Night

Topical retinoids (retinol, tretinoin, adapalene) are the most evidence-backed anti-aging active. Apply pea-sized amounts to clean, dry skin at night. Start 2× weekly, build to nightly over 8 weeks. Avoid during pregnancy — consult your dermatologist for safe alternatives.

💧
Barrier Function First

A compromised barrier — redness, flaking, sensitivity — undermines every treatment you apply. Prioritise ceramide-rich moisturisers, gentle pH-balanced cleansers (pH 4.5–5.5), and avoid over-exfoliation. Rebuild the barrier before layering any active ingredients.

🍊
Vitamin C in the Morning

L-ascorbic acid at 15–20% concentration neutralises free radicals, brightens post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation and potentiates your SPF up to 4×. Pair with ferulic acid for stability. Store in a dark, cool place and replace when it turns orange or yellow.

🛌
Sleep & Skin Repair

During deep sleep, growth hormone triggers peak collagen synthesis and cellular turnover. Chronic sleep deprivation raises cortisol, degrading collagen and aggravating acne and eczema. Aim for 7–9 hours on a silk pillowcase to reduce sleep-crease formation.

🥗
Diet & Skin Health

High glycaemic foods spike insulin and promote IGF-1, driving sebum and acne. An anti-inflammatory diet rich in omega-3s (salmon, walnuts) and antioxidants (berries, leafy greens), low in dairy, measurably improves acne and radiance within 10–12 weeks.

FAQ

Common Questions About Your Skin

How many sessions will I need? ＋
Acne protocols typically run 8–12 weeks. Laser resurfacing courses are 3–6 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart. Anti-aging injectables are repeated every 3–6 months. You'll receive a written treatment schedule at your consultation with realistic timelines and expected outcomes at each stage.
Are the treatments painful? ＋
Most patients describe laser treatments as a mild warm snapping sensation. We apply topical anaesthetic 30–45 minutes beforehand. Injectables use ultra-fine needles and most contain lidocaine in the formula. Chemical peels produce mild tingling only. Patient comfort is always our priority.
What is the downtime after laser treatment? ＋
Ablative fractional CO₂ involves 5–7 days of redness and peeling; most patients return to work by day 7 with mineral makeup. Non-ablative lasers and IPL have minimal to no downtime — mild pinkness for a few hours. We provide a written aftercare guide and recovery skincare kit with every laser treatment.
Is treatment safe for darker skin tones? ＋
Absolutely. Dr. Mitchell specialises in Fitzpatrick types III–VI. We use PicoSure, long-pulsed Nd:YAG and calibrated IPL protocols proven safe for melanin-rich skin. We never use outdated technologies or one-size-fits-all settings — every parameter is personalised to your skin type.
Can I combine multiple treatments? ＋
Yes — combination therapy often delivers superior results. We commonly pair fractional laser with PRP, botulinum toxin with skin-boosting injections, and chemical peels with microneedling. Your written plan will specify safe timing and sequencing for optimal outcomes.
Do you offer prescription skincare? ＋
Yes. As a medical clinic we prescribe pharmaceutical-grade topicals unavailable over the counter — including tretinoin, oral antibiotics, azelaic acid 20%, and compounded formulations tailored to your needs. Prescriptions are reviewed at every follow-up and adjusted as your skin evolves.
